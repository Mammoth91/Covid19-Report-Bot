<!-- Tous les autres frames --><div id="c46333" class=" frame frame-default frame-type-textmedia frame-layout-0"><a id="c46496"></a><h2 class="">
				Situation in Québec and worldwide
			</h2><div class="ce-textpic ce-left ce-above"></div></div>


<!-- Tous les autres frames --><div id="c46335" class=" frame frame-avis frame-type-textmedia frame-layout-0"><a id="c46497"></a><div class="ce-textpic ce-left ce-above"><div class="ce-bodytext"><p>The World Health Organization has officially declared COVID-19 a pandemic.</p><p>For now, the spread of the coronavirus (COVID-19) is under control in Québec, but the coming weeks will be critical. The Government is taking all the necessary measures to contain the contagion as much as possible.</p></div></div></div>


<!-- Tous les autres frames --><div id="c46337" class=" frame frame-default frame-type-textmedia frame-layout-0"><a id="c46498"></a><div class="ce-textpic ce-left ce-above"><div class="ce-bodytext"><p>In <strong>Québec</strong>, there are now 17<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>confirmed cases of&nbsp;COVID‑19.</p><div class = "tableauOverflow"><table class="contenttable"><thead><tr><th><p>Regions</p></th><th><p class="texteCentre">Number of new confirmed cases<br /> 			(on March<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>13,<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>3<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>p.m.)</p></th><th><p class="texteCentre">Total number of confirmed cases by region</p></th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><p>04- Mauricie - Centre du Québec</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">0</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">1</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>05 - Estrie</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">0</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">2</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>06 - Montréal</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">0</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">7</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>12- Chaudière-Appalaches</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">0</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">1</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>15 - Laurentides</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">0</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">2</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>16- Montérégie</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">0</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">4</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Total</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">0</p></td><td><p class="texteCentre">17</p></td></tr></tbody></table></div><p>At the moment, all the people who are infected had travelled abroad.</p><p>In <strong>Canada</strong>, the overall risk remains low. <!-- Tous les autres frames --><div id="c46339" class=" frame frame-default frame-type-textmedia frame-layout-0"><a id="c46499"></a><h2 class="">
				Measures taken by the Government
			</h2><div class="ce-textpic ce-left ce-above"><div class="ce-bodytext"><p>The Government has taken a number of measures to contain the spread, including:</p><ul><li><a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/public-health/services/diseases/2019-novel-coronavirus-infection/health-professionals/interim-guidance-cases-contacts.html" target="_blank">self-isolation&nbsp;<img class="linkExtImg" src="/typo3conf/ext/gabarit_pgu/Resources/Public/Images/Templates/lien-externe.svg" alt="This hyperlink will open in a new window." /></a>&nbsp;for 14<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>days for anyone who, as of March<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>12, 2020, returns from abroad.</li><li><a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/public-health/services/diseases/2019-novel-coronavirus-infection/health-professionals/interim-guidance-cases-contacts.html" target="_blank">mandatory isolation&nbsp;<img class="linkExtImg" src="/typo3conf/ext/gabarit_pgu/Resources/Public/Images/Templates/lien-externe.svg" alt="This hyperlink will open in a new window." /></a>&nbsp;for 14<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>days for all public service employees and health care, education and daycare workers, both private and public, who, as of March<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>12, 2020, return from abroad;</li><li>the cancellation, as of March<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>12, 2020 and for 30<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>days, of indoor gatherings of more than 250<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>people or unnecessary gatherings.</li><li>the closing of all childcare services (childcare centres, subsidized and non-subsidized childcare services, home childcare services and unregulated childcare services) and the education network (elementary and secondary schools, training centres, private schools, CEGEPs, colleges and universities) from Monday, March<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>16 to Friday, March<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>27. However, measures will be implemented to continue to offer childcare services to parents who work in the health network and in essential services: 	<ul><li><p>prevention and control in health care settings in suspected or confirmed cases of infection.</p></li></ul></li></ul><p>Health care professionals in Québec, in particular doctors, nurses, laboratory staff and paramedics, have access to tools to quickly identify cases suspicious for COVID-19, ensure they are managed appropriately and that the necessary prevention and control measures are taken.</p></div></div></div>


<!-- Tous les autres frames --><div id="c46341" class=" frame frame-default frame-type-textmedia frame-layout-0"><a id="c46500"></a><h2 class="">
				Health recommendations
			</h2><div class="ce-textpic ce-left ce-above"><div class="ce-bodytext"><h3>For citizens returning from travelling</h3><p><a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/public-health/services/diseases/2019-novel-coronavirus-infection/health-professionals/interim-guidance-cases-contacts.html#app1" target="_blank">Self-isolation&nbsp;<img class="linkExtImg" src="/typo3conf/ext/gabarit_pgu/Resources/Public/Images/Templates/lien-externe.svg" alt="This hyperlink will open in a new window." /></a>&nbsp;for 14<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>days is recommended for anyone returning from abroad as of March<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>12, 2020. Returning travellers must be vigilant and monitor themselves for symptoms.</p><p><a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/public-health/services/diseases/2019-novel-coronavirus-infection/health-professionals/interim-guidance-cases-contacts.html#app1" target="_blank">Self-isolation&nbsp;<img class="linkExtImg" src="/typo3conf/ext/gabarit_pgu/Resources/Public/Images/Templates/lien-externe.svg" alt="This hyperlink will open in a new window." /></a>&nbsp;is mandatory for all public service employees and health care, education and daycare workers, both private and public, returning from abroad. Public sector employees will continue their treatment during the isolation period.</p><p>Residents of Quebec who develop symptoms of fever, cough or difficulty breathing when they return from travelling abroad must contact the toll-free line <a href="tel:+1-877-644-4545">1&nbsp;877&nbsp;644-4545</a>.</p><h3>For citizens who are not returning from travelling but who have symptoms</h3><p>Anyone who, as of March<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>12, 2020, develops flu- or cold-like symptoms must&nbsp;<a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/public-health/services/diseases/2019-novel-coronavirus-infection/health-professionals/interim-guidance-cases-contacts.html#app1" target="_blank">self-isolate&nbsp;<img class="linkExtImg" src="/typo3conf/ext/gabarit_pgu/Resources/Public/Images/Templates/lien-externe.svg" alt="This hyperlink will open in a new window." /></a> until the symptoms have resolved. Home isolation is not required for other household members who do not have symptoms.</p><p>People who are uncertain about their health can contact the toll-free line <a href="tel:+1-877-644-4545">1&nbsp;877&nbsp;644-4545</a>&nbsp;first. A nurse will assess the situation and provide appropriate recommendations.</p><h3>For everyone</h3><ul><li>Wash your hands often with soap under warm running water for at least 20 seconds.</li><li>Use an alcohol-based hand rub if soap and water are not available.</li><li>Practice proper cough and sneeze etiquette: 	<ul><li>Cover your mouth and nose with your arm to reduce the spread of germs.</li><li>If you use a tissue, dispose of it as soon as possible and wash your hands afterwards.</li></ul></li><li>If you are sick, avoid contact with more vulnerable people, including older adults and people who have a chronic disease. For example, avoid visiting people in hospital, people living in residential and long-term care centres or in private residences.</li><li>Avoid direct contact when you greet someone, such as shaking hands, and use alternative ways to greet people.</li></ul><p>Every individual has their part to play to reduce the risk of spreading viruses.</p><p>Masks are not an effective means of protection for the general public. Their use is indicated more for patients who are suspected of being infected and for the health professionals who are providing care for them.</p><p>If you are worried or anxious about the coronavirus, you can contact the toll-free line 1&nbsp;877&nbsp;644-4545.</p></div></div></div>


<!-- Tous les autres frames --><div id="c46349" class=" frame frame-default frame-type-textmedia frame-layout-0"><a id="c46501"></a><h2 class="">
				Symptoms and treatments
			</h2><div class="ce-textpic ce-left ce-above"><div class="ce-bodytext"><p>The main symptoms of COVID-19 are as follows:</p><ul><li>Fever</li><li>Cough</li><li>Difficulty breathing</li></ul><p>The symptoms can be mild (similar to a cold) or more severe (such as those associated with pneumonia and respiratory or kidney failure).</p><p>In rare cases, infection can lead to death. People most at risk of complications are those with a weakened immune system or a chronic disease and older people.</p><h3>Treatment</h3><p>There is no specific treatment or vaccine for COVID-19. Supportive treatment may, however, be provided.</p><p>Most people with COVID-19 will recover on their own.</p><p>For more general information about COVID-19, go to the page <a href="/en/health/health-issues/a-z/general-information-about-coronavirus/">COVID-19 – General information</a>.</p></div></div></div>


<!-- Tous les autres frames --><div id="c46459" class=" frame frame-default frame-type-textmedia frame-layout-0"><a id="c46502"></a><h2 class="">
				Education and childcare services networks
			</h2><div class="ce-textpic ce-left ce-above"><div class="ce-bodytext"><p>The Québec government has announced the closing, from Monday, March 16 to Friday, March 27, of all childcare services (childcare centres, subsidized and non-subsidized childcare services, home childcare services and unregulated childcare services) and the education network (elementary and secondary schools, training centres, private schools, CEGEPs, colleges and universities).</p><p>However, measures will be implemented to continue to offer childcare services to parents who work in the health network and in essential services.&nbsp;</p></div></div></div>


<!-- Tous les autres frames --><div id="c46353" class=" frame frame-default frame-type-textmedia frame-layout-0"><a id="c46503"></a><h2 class="">
				Events
			</h2><div class="ce-textpic ce-left ce-above"><div class="ce-bodytext"><p>As of March 12, 2020, the Gouvernement du&nbsp;Québec is banning all indoor gatherings of more than 250 people for 30 days.</p><p>Cancelling unnecessary gatherings is also strongly recommended to reduce the risk of spreading the coronavirus.</p></div></div></div>


<!-- Tous les autres frames --><div id="c46355" class=" frame frame-default frame-type-textmedia frame-layout-0"><a id="c46504"></a><h2 class="">
				Measures to be taken by businesses
			</h2><div class="ce-textpic ce-left ce-above"><div class="ce-bodytext"><p>Employers are asked to be flexible regarding their employees’ work schedules to limit, for example, crowds in public transit during rush hour.</p><p>If possible, employers are also asked to encourage their employees to telework.</p><p>Measures will be announced shortly to support businesses who are experiencing cash-flow problems due to the impact of the coronavirus.</p><p>Businesses that deliver goods from abroad in Québec do not have to ask their employees to temporarily self-isolate.</p></div></div></div>


<!-- Tous les autres frames --><div id="c46383" class=" frame frame-default frame-type-textmedia frame-layout-0"><a id="c46505"></a><h2 class="">
				Frequently asked questions
			</h2><div class="ce-textpic ce-left ce-above"><div class="ce-bodytext"><h3>1. What are the main symptoms associated with COVID-19?</h3><p>The main symptoms of COVID-19 are as follows:</p><ul><li>Fever</li><li>Cough</li><li>Difficulty breathing</li></ul><p>The symptoms can be mild (similar to a cold) or more severe (such as those associated with pneumonia and respiratory or kidney failure).</p><h3>2. How is COVID-19 spread?</h3><p>COVID-19 usually infects the nose, throat and lungs. In most cases, it is spread by:</p><ul><li>close contact with an infected person when the person coughs or sneezes;</li><li>touching infected surfaces with your hands and then touching your mouth, nose or eyes.</li></ul><h3>3. How long can COVID-19 survive on objects?</h3><p>In general, COVID-19 does not survive for long on objects:</p><ul><li>3 hours on inert objects with dry surfaces;</li><li>6 days on inert objects with wet surfaces.</li></ul><h3>4. Will wearing a mask protect me effectively against COVID-19?</h3><p>Masks are not an effective means of protection for the general public in Québec. Their use is indicated more for patients who are suspected of being infected and for the health professionals who are providing care for them.</p><h3>5. Is there any treatment for COVID-19?</h3><p>There is no specific treatment or vaccine for COVID-19 for the moment. Most people with the virus will recover on their own.</p><h3>6. Which countries are most at risk?</h3><p>For this information, you are advised to consult the&nbsp;<a href="https://travel.gc.ca/travelling/advisories?_ga=2.105850506.886082967.1584121752-1553186751.1579968697" target="_blank">Travel Advice and Advisories&nbsp;<img class="linkExtImg" src="/typo3conf/ext/gabarit_pgu/Resources/Public/Images/Templates/lien-externe.svg" alt="This hyperlink will open in a new window." /></a>&nbsp;section on the Government of Canada site.</p><h3>7. I have travel plans. Should I cancel them?</h3><p>The Gouvernement du Québec&nbsp;recommends cancelling all non-essential travel.</p><p>If you decide to travel anyway, you are advised to consult the&nbsp;<a href="https://travel.gc.ca/travelling/advisories?_ga=2.105850506.886082967.1584121752-1553186751.1579968697" target="_blank">Travel Advice and Advisories&nbsp;<img class="linkExtImg" src="/typo3conf/ext/gabarit_pgu/Resources/Public/Images/Templates/lien-externe.svg" alt="This hyperlink will open in a new window." /></a>&nbsp;section on the Government of Canada site. You must also self-isolate for 14 days when you return.</p><h3>8. I am returning from a trip. What do I have to do?</h3><p><a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/public-health/services/diseases/2019-novel-coronavirus-infection/health-professionals/interim-guidance-cases-contacts.html" target="_blank">Self-isolation&nbsp;<img class="linkExtImg" src="/typo3conf/ext/gabarit_pgu/Resources/Public/Images/Templates/lien-externe.svg" alt="This hyperlink will open in a new window." /></a>&nbsp;for 14 days is recommended for anyone returning from abroad of March 12, 2020. Returning travellers must be vigilant and monitor themselves for symptoms.</p><p><a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/public-health/services/diseases/2019-novel-coronavirus-infection/health-professionals/interim-guidance-cases-contacts.html" target="_blank">Self-isolation&nbsp;<img class="linkExtImg" src="/typo3conf/ext/gabarit_pgu/Resources/Public/Images/Templates/lien-externe.svg" alt="This hyperlink will open in a new window." /></a>&nbsp;is mandatory for all public service employees and health care, education and daycare workers, both private and public, returning from abroad; Public sector employees will continue their treatment during the isolation period.</p><h3>9. I am not returning from a trip, but I have flu- or cold-like symptoms. What do I have to do?</h3><p>Residents of Quebec who develop symptoms of fever, cough or difficulty breathing after returning from travelling abroad must contact <a href="tel:+1-877-644-4545">1 877 644-4545</a>.</p><p>Anyone who, as of March 12, 2020, develops flu- or cold-like symptoms must&nbsp;<a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/public-health/services/diseases/2019-novel-coronavirus-infection/health-professionals/interim-guidance-cases-contacts.html" target="_blank">self-isolate&nbsp;<img class="linkExtImg" src="/typo3conf/ext/gabarit_pgu/Resources/Public/Images/Templates/lien-externe.svg" alt="This hyperlink will open in a new window." /></a> until the symptoms have resolved. Home isolation is not required for other household members who do not have any symptoms.</p><h3>10. I work in the private sector and I have to self-isolate. Can my employer fire me?</h3><p>The Gouvernement du Québec&nbsp;is asking employers to be understanding and flexible toward employees who are returning from travelling or who have symptoms.</p><h3>11.&nbsp;Should I be worried about the COVID-19 situation?</h3><p>For now, the spread of COVID-19 is under control in Québec, but the situation must be monitored closely. The Government is taking all the necessary measures to contain the contagion as much as possible.</p><h3>12. What is self-isolation?</h3><p><strong>Self-isolation</strong> is when an infected or potentially infected person is asked to stay at home and monitor themselves for symptoms during the virus’s incubation period. You can find out more about recommended practices during self-isolation by visiting the page&nbsp;<a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/public-health/services/diseases/2019-novel-coronavirus-infection/health-professionals/interim-guidance-cases-contacts.html" target="_blank">Instructions for self-Isolating in the home or co-living setting&nbsp;<img class="linkExtImg" src="/typo3conf/ext/gabarit_pgu/Resources/Public/Images/Templates/lien-externe.svg" alt="This hyperlink will open in a new window." /></a>&nbsp;on the Government of Canada’s website.</p><h3>13. Which childcare services are closing?</h3><p>All childcare services will be closed from Monday, March<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>16 to Friday, March<span class="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>27. The closing covers childcare centres and subsidized and non-subsidized childcare services, home childcare services, and unregulated childcare services.</p><p>However, measures will be implemented to continue to offer childcare services to parents who work in the health network and in essential services.</p><h3>14. What must parents who work in the health network and whose children attend childcare services do?</h3><p>Measures will be implemented to continue to offer childcare services to parents who work in the health network and in essential services.</p><h3>15. Must parents continue to pay the childcare service?</h3><p>No. The government will pay the parental contribution and the childcare service subsidy for the entire two weeks.</p><h3>16. Will non-subsidized daycare centres be compensated?</h3><p>Yes. Measures will be announced shortly.</p><h3>17.&nbsp;Which educational services are closing?</h3><p>All services offered in the education network will be closed from Monday, March 16 to Friday, March 27. The closing concerns elementary and secondary schools, training centres, private schools, CEGEPs and colleges and universities.</p><h3>18. Can COVID-19 cause a food shortage?</h3><p>No. There is no reason to fear a food shortage.</p></div></div></div>
